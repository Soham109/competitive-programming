# Obtain Two Zeroes

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/1260/B)

## Idea

The key observation is that the operations preserve two invariants: (1) the sum $a + b$ decreases by $3x$ for any $x$, so $a + b$ must be divisible by $3$ to reach $0$, and (2) neither $a$ nor $b$ can fall below zero, which means the larger value must not exceed twice the smaller value at any step (i.e., $\max(a, b) \leq 2 \cdot \min(a, b)$). These two conditions are both necessary and sufficient to determine whether $(a, b)$ can be reduced to $(0, 0)$.

## Approach

1. Check if $a + b$ is divisible by $3$. This is required because every operation reduces $a + b$ by $3x$, and reaching $0$ is impossible otherwise.
2. Verify that $\max(a, b) \leq 2 \cdot \min(a, b)$. This ensures that neither $a$ nor $b$ becomes negative during the sequence of operations. Intuitively, this condition arises because the larger value decreases more slowly in each operation, so it must not grow disproportionately large compared to the smaller value.
3. If both conditions are satisfied, output "YES"; otherwise, output "NO".

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(1)$ per test case, as both conditions can be checked in constant time. For $t$ test cases, the total complexity is $O(t)$.
- **Space:** $O(1)$, as no additional space is used beyond a constant number of variables.

## Notes

- The condition $\max(a, b) \leq 2 \cdot \min(a, b)$ can also be interpreted geometrically: the operations must keep $(a, b)$ within a triangular region bounded by the axes and the line $a = 2b$ (or $b = 2a$).
