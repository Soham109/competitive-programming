# Little Elephant and Numbers

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/221/B)

## Idea

The key observation is that a divisor $d$ of $x$ shares at least one digit with $x$ if and only if there exists at least one digit in $d$ that also appears in $x$. This is because $d$ being a divisor of $x$ implies that $d$ is a number derived from the same numerical base as $x$, and we can directly compare their decimal digits. Therefore, the problem reduces to checking the intersection of the digit sets of $x$ and each of its divisors.

## Approach

1. **Find all divisors of $x$:** A number $d$ is a divisor of $x$ if $x \mod d = 0$. To efficiently find all divisors, iterate $d$ from $1$ to $\sqrt{x}$, adding both $d$ and $x/d$ to the list of divisors whenever $d$ divides $x$.

2. **Extract digits of $x$:** Use modular arithmetic to extract the digits of $x$ and store them in a set or map for efficient lookup.

3. **Check divisors for common digits:** For each divisor $d$ of $x$, extract its digits and check if any of them exist in the digit set of $x$. If a common digit is found, increment the count of valid divisors.

4. **Output the result:** The final count represents the number of divisors of $x$ that share at least one digit with $x$.

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(\sqrt{x} \cdot \log x)$ — Computing divisors takes $O(\sqrt{x})$, and for each divisor, extracting and comparing digits takes $O(\log x)$.
- **Space:** $O(\log x)$ — Storing the digits of $x$ and a single divisor requires space proportional to the number of digits in $x$.

## Notes

- The digit extraction and comparison are efficiently handled using a `map` or `set` data structure, ensuring that the solution remains performant even for the upper limit of $x$.
