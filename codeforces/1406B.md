# Maximum Product

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/1406/B)

## Idea

The key insight is that the maximum product of five numbers in an array can only involve either the largest positive numbers or the smallest (most negative) numbers. This is because multiplying two negative numbers results in a positive number, which can potentially contribute to a larger product when combined with other large numbers. Thus, the problem reduces to considering combinations of the largest positive numbers and the smallest negative numbers to maximize the product.

## Approach

1. **Classify numbers into positives, negatives, and zeros:** Separate the input array into three groups — positive numbers, negative numbers, and zeros. This allows us to focus only on the numbers that can contribute to the product (positives and negatives).

2. **Sort the groups:** Sort the positive numbers in descending order and the negative numbers in ascending order (most negative to least negative). This ensures that the largest and smallest numbers are easily accessible.

3. **Handle edge cases:** If the array contains fewer than five non-zero numbers, the product is zero because at least one of the numbers in the product will be zero. If there are no positive numbers, the product must involve only negative numbers.

4. **Evaluate candidate combinations:** Compute the product for all valid combinations of five numbers:
   - The five largest positive numbers (if there are at least five positives).
   - The three largest positive numbers and the two smallest (most negative) numbers (if there are at least three positives and two negatives).
   - The largest positive number and the four smallest (most negative) numbers (if there is at least one positive and four negatives).
   - If there are zeros in the array, consider the product of zero as a valid candidate (since it may be the maximum product when all other combinations are negative).

5. **Output the maximum product:** Among all the computed candidate products, the maximum value is the answer for the test case.

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(n \log n)$ per test case due to sorting the positive and negative numbers. Across all test cases, the total complexity is $O(T \cdot n \log n)$, where $T$ is the number of test cases and $n$ is the size of the array.
- **Space:** $O(n)$ per test case for storing the positive and negative numbers.

## Notes

- The solution ensures correctness by explicitly handling edge cases such as arrays with zeros, no positive numbers, or no negative numbers. 
- Care must be taken to handle large products, so the implementation uses `long long` to avoid overflow.
