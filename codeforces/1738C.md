# Even Number Addicts

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/1738/C)

## Idea

The key observation is that the parity (odd/even nature) of the sum of Alice's selected integers is determined by the parity of the total number of odd integers in the sequence. This is because the parity of the sum of even numbers does not change, while the parity of the sum of odd numbers flips each time an odd number is added. Therefore, the winner is determined by how the players strategically select numbers to control the parity of Alice's sum, given the total counts of odd and even numbers.

## Approach

1. Count the number of odd integers ($n_o$) and even integers ($n_e$) in the sequence. Only the count of odd numbers affects the parity of Alice's sum, as adding even numbers does not change the sum's parity.

2. Observe that the game state depends on $n_o \pmod{4}$:
   - If $n_o \equiv 0 \pmod{4}$, Alice can always win by ensuring her sum remains even.
   - If $n_o \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, Bob can win if $n_e$ is even, as he can force Alice's sum to become odd. If $n_e$ is odd, Alice can still win by using the last even number to maintain an even sum.
   - If $n_o \equiv 2 \pmod{4}$, Bob can always win by forcing Alice's sum to become odd.
   - If $n_o \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$, Alice can always win by forcing Bob into a losing position.

3. Based on the above, compute $n_o \pmod{4}$ and check the parity of $n_e$ when $n_o \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$. Use this to determine the winner for each test case.

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(t \cdot n)$, where $t$ is the number of test cases and $n$ is the maximum sequence length. Counting odd and even numbers in each sequence requires $O(n)$ time.
- **Space:** $O(n)$ for storing the sequence of integers in each test case.

## Notes

- The modulo operation $n_o \pmod{4}$ is sufficient to determine the game's outcome, as the parity of Alice's sum depends only on the sequence of moves and the parity of the remaining odd numbers.
