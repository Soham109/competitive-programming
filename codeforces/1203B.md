# Equal Rectangles

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/1203/B)

## Idea

The key observation is that for $n$ rectangles to have equal area, each rectangle must consist of two pairs of sticks with the same length, and the product of the lengths of these pairs must be identical across all rectangles. This is possible only if the sticks can be grouped into $2n$ pairs of equal length, and the products of the smallest and largest sticks in each pair must remain constant across all pairs. Sorting the stick lengths simplifies the pairing process and ensures that the largest and smallest sticks are paired optimally.

## Approach

1. **Count stick frequencies:** For the sticks to be paired into $2n$ groups of equal lengths, every stick length must appear an even number of times. If any stick length appears an odd number of times, output "NO".

2. **Sort the sticks:** Sort the stick lengths in ascending order. This allows us to pair the smallest stick with the largest stick, the second smallest with the second largest, and so on. This pairing minimizes the risk of mismatched areas.

3. **Compute the target area:** Calculate the area of the first rectangle using the product of the smallest and largest stick lengths. This area will serve as the target area for all rectangles.

4. **Validate pair products:** Iterate through the sorted stick lengths, pairing the smallest and largest remaining sticks. For each pair, compute the product of their lengths. If any product differs from the target area, or if the number of pairs is not exactly $2n$, output "NO".

5. **Output the result:** If all pairs have been validated and the conditions are met, output "YES".

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(4n \log (4n))$ per query, dominated by the sorting step.
- **Space:** $O(4n)$ for storing the stick lengths.

## Notes

- Sorting simplifies the pairing process and ensures that the largest and smallest sticks are paired optimally, minimizing the chance of mismatched areas.
- The use of a `map` to count stick frequencies ensures that we can quickly check the evenness condition for all stick lengths.
