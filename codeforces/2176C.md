# Odd Process

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/2176/C)

## Idea

The key insight is that the cat's behavior depends entirely on the parity of the sum of the coins in the bag. Specifically, the bag is emptied whenever the sum becomes even. Therefore, to maximize the final score, we must carefully select coins to ensure that the sum remains odd after each action. This is best achieved by prioritizing the largest odd coins first, and only using even coins when necessary to maintain the odd sum.

## Approach

1. **Separate coins by parity:** Split the coins into two groups: `o` (odd coins) and `e` (even coins). This is because the parity of the sum is solely determined by the parity of the coins added to the bag.

2. **Sort coins in descending order:** Sort both `o` and `e` in descending order. This allows us to always pick the largest available coin of a given parity, which is optimal for maximizing the score.

3. **Handle edge cases:** If all coins are even, the bag will always be emptied after every addition, resulting in a score of $0$ for all $k$. If all coins are odd, the score alternates between the largest remaining odd coin for odd $k$ and $0$ for even $k$.

4. **Iterate over $k$ actions:** For each $k$ from $1$ to $n$, determine the maximum possible score:
   - If $k$ is odd, add the largest remaining odd coin to the bag (if available).
   - If $k$ is even, add the largest remaining even coin to the bag (if available) to maintain the odd sum.
   - If no suitable coin is available, the score for that $k$ will be the same as the score for $k-2$ (since the bag would have been emptied).

5. **Output the results:** For each test case, output the maximum scores for all $k$ from $1$ to $n$.

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(n \log n)$ per test case, where $n$ is the number of coins. This is dominated by the sorting step.
- **Space:** $O(n)$ to store the separated `o` and `e` arrays.

## Notes

- If the number of odd coins is even, the final sum for $k = n$ will always be $0$ because the total sum of all coins will be even and the bag will be emptied.
- The solution avoids overflow by carefully managing the addition of large integers and by using `long long` for all calculations.
