# Loyalty

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/2161/C)

## Idea

The key insight is that the number of bonus points earned by purchasing an item depends on whether the purchase increases the loyalty level. The loyalty level increases when the total cost $S$ of items purchased so far crosses a multiple of $X$. To maximize bonus points, we should prioritize purchases that either (1) bring $S$ closer to the next multiple of $X$ without exceeding it or (2) directly push $S$ past the next multiple of $X$. Sorting the items by price and carefully choosing the order of purchases ensures the maximum number of loyalty level increases.

## Approach

1. **Sort items by price:** Start by sorting the items in non-decreasing order. This allows us to process smaller prices first, which are easier to fit into the remaining gap before the next multiple of $X$.

2. **Track the current total cost $S$ and the next loyalty threshold:** Initialize $S = 0$. For each item, calculate the gap to the next multiple of $X$: $gap = X - (S \bmod X)$.

3. **Choose the optimal item to purchase next:**
   - If the most expensive remaining item (highest price) can push $S$ past the next multiple of $X$, buy it immediately to earn bonus points.
   - Otherwise, buy the cheapest remaining item to increase $S$ without overshooting the next loyalty threshold, setting up future purchases to cross the threshold efficiently.

4. **Iterate until all items are purchased:** Use two pointers to maintain the sorted list of items. One pointer starts at the smallest price, and the other at the largest price. Choose the next item to purchase based on the above logic, updating $S$ and tracking the order of purchases.

5. **Output the results:** After processing all items, output the total bonus points earned and the order of purchases.

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(n \log n)$ per test case, dominated by the sorting step. Across all test cases, the total complexity is $O(T \cdot n \log n)$, where $T$ is the number of test cases and $n$ is the total number of items across all test cases (bounded by $10^5$).
- **Space:** $O(n)$ per test case for storing the sorted list of item prices.

## Notes

- Sorting the items is crucial for efficiently determining the optimal order of purchases.
- The two-pointer approach ensures that we can dynamically choose between the smallest and largest remaining items, maximizing bonus points while maintaining linear time complexity after sorting.
