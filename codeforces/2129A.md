# Double Perspective

> [Problem on Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/2129/A)

## Idea

The key insight is to maximize $f(S') - g(S')$ by carefully selecting pairs to maximize the union of intervals while avoiding the creation of cycles in the graph. This can be achieved by greedily selecting pairs based on their interval endpoints, prioritizing those with larger upper bounds ($b_i$) and smaller lower bounds ($a_i$). This ensures that we maximize $f(S')$ while minimizing the risk of forming cycles that contribute to $g(S')$.

## Approach

1. **Reformulate the problem:** Recognize that $f(S')$ is maximized by selecting intervals that collectively cover the largest range on the number line, while $g(S')$ is minimized by avoiding cycles in the graph representation of $S'$.

2. **Sort the pairs:** Sort the pairs $\{(a_i, b_i)\}$ in descending order of their upper bounds $b_i$. For pairs with the same $b_i$, sort them by ascending order of $a_i$. This ensures that we prioritize intervals with larger ranges while avoiding overlaps that could lead to cycles.

3. **Iteratively select pairs:** Initialize a variable `glo` to a very large value, representing the smallest $a_i$ of the selected intervals. Traverse the sorted list of pairs, and for each pair $(a_i, b_i)$, include it in $S'$ only if $a_i < glo$. If the pair is selected, update `glo` to $a_i$.

4. **Output the result:** Keep track of the indices of the selected pairs and output them as the solution.

This approach ensures that the selected subset $S'$ maximizes $f(S')$ while avoiding cycles, which guarantees $g(S') = 0$. As a result, $f(S') - g(S')$ is maximized.

## Complexity

- **Time:** $O(n \log n)$ per test case for sorting the pairs, plus $O(n)$ for selecting the subset. Since the total $n^2$ across all test cases is bounded by $9 \cdot 10^6$, the overall complexity is $O(T \cdot n \log n)$, where $T$ is the number of test cases.
- **Space:** $O(n)$ to store the sorted pairs and the selected indices.

## Notes

- Sorting by descending $b_i$ and then by ascending $a_i$ ensures that we prioritize intervals with larger ranges and avoid overlapping intervals that could form cycles.
- The use of a single variable `glo` to track the smallest $a_i$ of the selected intervals is a key implementation detail that ensures the correctness of the greedy approach.
